Border Security and Regional Cooperation

Border security and regional cooperation are vital components in the fight against terrorism. Strengthening border security involves implementing measures to prevent the illegal movement of individuals, weapons, and contraband across borders

Our Mission

Our mission is to reduce insecurity and prevent terror attacks in northeastern Kenya through innovative strategies, community engagement, and collaborative efforts. We are dedicated to creating a safer, more secure environment for all residents, fostering peace, and promoting sustainable development in the region.

Our Vision

Our vision is to create a safe, secure, and thriving northeastern Kenya where communities live free from fear and the threat of terrorism. We aim to foster a region marked by peace, stability, and sustainable development, where every individual can pursue their aspirations without the shadow of insecurity.

Crisis Management and Response

Emergency Preparedness: Developing and regularly updating emergency response plans for various types of terrorist attacks is essential. This includes training first responders and ensuring the availability of necessary resources.

Anti-Terrorism Legislation

Enacting laws that define and penalize terrorist activities, including the financing and support of terrorism, is essential. These laws should also ensure the protection of human rights to maintain public trust.

Attacks in Garissa: towards the precipice

Warning Off
Marginalisation

Garissa is a borderland. It is hard to understand the peculiar risks it faces without understanding its history. It is both peripheral and significant within Kenya, a contested terrain in which intense rivalries play out between local people and across its boundaries.

Differentiate
local perceptions of violence

Shabaab’s attacks in Garissa followed a pattern familiar from elsewhere in Kenya. Aimed at businesses, churches, hotels, security forces and the university, they targeted non-Somali outsiders, who were chosen for reasons local people could identify with. In some cases, such as the killing of a prison guard near the Safari hotel, it is not clear whether an attack was the work of Shabaab or just a settling of local scores.

Rebuilding trust and unity

It has been observed in other contexts that terror tactics tend to provoke public revulsion that can be self-defeating as long as the response is not disproportionately brutal and indiscriminate. [81] This self-defeating tendency was certainly present in Garissa.

peacebuilding moment

Garissa has made undeniable progress. But as one local peace worker explained “Countering violent extremism is short term but peacebuilding is needed for the long term”. [104] This is by no means ‘CVE job done’. All the structural challenges faced by Garissa before the attacks still remain challenges now.
